# SME Classification and Support in Vietnam

**Based on:** Law 04/2017/QH14, Decree 80/2021/ND-CP, Decree 39/2019/ND-CP, Decree 45/2024/ND-CP
**Jurisdiction:** Vietnam

## SME Definition (Decree 80/2021/ND-CP)

Vietnam classifies enterprises by size using two criteria: number of employees AND annual revenue.

### Agriculture, Forestry, Fisheries
- **Micro:** ≤10 employees AND revenue ≤3 billion VND
- **Small:** ≤100 employees AND revenue ≤50 billion VND
- **Medium:** ≤200 employees AND revenue ≤200 billion VND

### Industry and Construction
- **Micro:** ≤10 employees AND revenue ≤3 billion VND
- **Small:** ≤100 employees AND revenue ≤50 billion VND
- **Medium:** ≤200 employees AND revenue ≤200 billion VND

### Trade and Services
- **Micro:** ≤10 employees AND revenue ≤10 billion VND
- **Small:** ≤50 employees AND revenue ≤100 billion VND
- **Medium:** ≤100 employees AND revenue ≤300 billion VND

## Key Statistics

- 98% of Vietnamese businesses are classified as SMEs
- SMEs contribute approximately 45% of GDP
- SMEs employ approximately 60% of the labor force
- Only 9.3% of SMEs can access bank credit
- $24 billion credit gap for SMEs in Vietnam
- 62% of SME financing needs are unmet

## SME Development Fund (Decree 39/2019, amended by Decree 45/2024)

- **Fund size:** VND 2,000 billion (approximately USD 80 million)
- **100% state-owned**
- Preferential lending rates (Decree 45/2024):
  - Short-term loans: 1.2% per annum
  - Medium/long-term loans: 4.4% per annum (80% of the lowest commercial bank rate)
  - Maximum loan tenor: 7 years
- **Eligibility:** SMEs investing in technology improvement or participating in value chains
- **Condition:** Must have repaid all previous Fund loans before applying for new ones

## Tax Incentives (Law 04/2017)

- SMEs with annual revenue below VND 20 billion: reduced corporate income tax rate
- Tax exemption for first 2 years for startups in priority sectors
- Accelerated depreciation for technology investments

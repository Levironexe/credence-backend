# SME Lending Best Practices for Vietnam

**Jurisdiction:** Vietnam
**Category:** Best Practice

## Financial Ratio Analysis for SME Creditworthiness

### Liquidity Ratios
- **Current Ratio** = Current Assets / Current Liabilities
  - Healthy: > 1.5 | Warning: < 1.0
- **Quick Ratio** = (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities
  - Healthy: > 1.0

### Leverage Ratios
- **Debt-to-Equity** = Total Debt / Shareholders' Equity
  - Low risk: < 1.0 | Medium risk: 1.0-2.0 | High risk: > 2.0
- **Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)** = Operating Income / Total Debt Service
  - Strong: > 1.5 | Minimum acceptable: > 1.25
  - Vietnamese banks typically require DSCR > 1.3 for SME loans

### Profitability Ratios
- **Gross Profit Margin** > 30% (healthy)
- **Net Profit Margin** > 10% (healthy)
- **Return on Equity (ROE)** > 15% (good), > 20% (excellent)

## SME Default Risk Indicators

### Financial Red Flags
- Declining revenue (>15% year-over-year decline)
- Negative cash flow for 3+ consecutive months
- Current ratio below 1.0
- Debt-to-equity above 2.5
- Missing or incomplete financial statements
- Frequent overdrafts or bounced checks

### Business Red Flags
- Less than 12 months in operation (startup risk)
- Heavy customer concentration (>50% revenue from single customer)
- Industry in structural decline
- Frequent changes in ownership or management
- Unresolved legal disputes or tax liens

### Positive Mitigating Factors
- Strong personal guarantee from creditworthy owner
- High-quality collateral (real estate, equipment with clear title)
- Long-term contracts with stable customers
- Proven resilience through previous economic downturns
- Consistent revenue growth over 2+ years

## Loan Pricing for SMEs

Vietnamese banks typically price SME loans at:
- **Short-term (< 12 months):** SBV base rate + 2-5% (currently 6-9% per annum)
- **Medium-term (1-5 years):** 9-12% per annum
- **Long-term (> 5 years):** 10-14% per annum
- **Priority sectors (agriculture, exports, high-tech):** Capped at 4.0% for short-term

## Collateral Requirements

### Types of Collateral Accepted
1. **Real Property:** Land use rights (most common), buildings. LTV 60-70% residential, 50-60% commercial.
2. **Movable Property:** Vehicles, machinery, inventory. LTV 40-60%.
3. **Financial Assets:** Bank deposits, government bonds, listed securities (50-60% haircut).
4. **Personal Guarantees:** Owner's personal guarantee, third-party guarantee.
5. **Government Guarantee:** Vietnam Development Bank covers up to 80% for eligible SMEs.

### Collateral Valuation
- Independent licensed valuation firm required
- Annual revaluation for Group 1 loans
- Semi-annual for Group 2-3 loans
- Quarterly for Group 4-5 loans

### Unsecured Lending
- Permitted but with higher risk weights (100% vs 75% for secured)
- Typically limited to short-term working capital
- Maximum often VND 500 million to VND 2 billion for SMEs
- Requires minimum 2 years of audited financial statements

## Default Prediction Framework

### Default Probability Thresholds
- < 5%: Low risk — standard terms, minimal monitoring
- 5-10%: Moderate risk — quarterly monitoring
- 10-20%: Elevated risk — monthly monitoring, may require collateral
- 20-30%: High risk — committee approval, strong collateral needed
- > 30%: Very high risk — typically declined

### Mapping to Loan Groups (Circular 11/2021)
- Default probability < 5% → Group 1 (Pass) — Provision: 0%
- Default probability 5-15% → Group 2 (Special Mention) — Provision: 5%
- Default probability 15-25% → Group 3 (Sub-standard) — Provision: 20%
- Default probability 25-50% → Group 4 (Doubtful) — Provision: 50%
- Default probability > 50% → Group 5 (Potential Loss) — Provision: 100%

## Early Warning Indicators

### Quantitative Signals
1. Declining account balances (>20% drop over 3 months)
2. Increasing frequency of late payments
3. Rising utilization of revolving credit lines (>80%)
4. Declining revenue trend visible in transaction data
5. Increase in returned checks or failed direct debits

### Qualitative Signals
1. Changes in key management personnel
2. Loss of major customer or contract
3. Negative industry news or regulatory actions
4. Customer becomes unresponsive to bank inquiries
5. Requests for loan restructuring or interest-only periods

# Vietnam Credit Information Center (CIC) — Credit Scoring Framework

**Based on:** Circular 03/2013/TT-NHNN and Circular 15/2023/TT-NHNN
**Jurisdiction:** Vietnam

The CIC is a unit under the State Bank of Vietnam responsible for collecting, storing, analyzing, and providing credit information of individuals and organizations.

## CIC Credit Score Scale: 150 to 750

### Score Bands

**150-250: Very Poor**
- Extremely high default risk (>50% probability)
- Recommended action: Decline or require exceptional mitigation

**251-350: Poor**
- High default risk (30-50% probability)
- Recommended action: Decline or require strong collateral + guarantor

**351-450: Below Average**
- Elevated default risk (15-30% probability)
- Recommended action: Manual review, enhanced monitoring, higher interest rate

**451-550: Average**
- Moderate default risk (8-15% probability)
- Recommended action: Standard approval with adequate collateral

**551-650: Good**
- Low default risk (3-8% probability)
- Recommended action: Standard approval, competitive rates

**651-750: Excellent**
- Very low default risk (<3% probability)
- Recommended action: Streamlined approval, best available rates

## CIC Data Collection

All credit institutions must report to CIC monthly.

Report includes: borrower identity, loan amount, outstanding balance, classification group, collateral value.

CIC maintains history for at least 5 years after loan closure.

Individuals and organizations can request their own CIC report.

## CIC Report Contents

1. Borrower identification information
2. Current outstanding loans across all institutions
3. Historical loan classification (Group 1-5) over time
4. Number of credit inquiries in the last 12 months
5. Credit score (150-750 scale)
6. Any defaults, restructurings, or write-offs

## Mandatory CIC Check

Credit institutions MUST check CIC before extending any new credit. Failure to check CIC before lending is a regulatory violation subject to penalties.

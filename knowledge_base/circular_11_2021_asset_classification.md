# Circular 11/2021/TT-NHNN — Asset Classification and Risk Provisioning

**Issued:** July 30, 2021
**Effective:** October 1, 2021
**Jurisdiction:** Vietnam

This circular establishes the framework for classifying loan assets and setting aside provisions for potential losses.

## 5-Group Loan Classification System

### Group 1 — Pass (No pha thuong)
- Loans within their original term, not restructured
- The borrower is assessed as having full capacity to repay
- **Provisioning rate: 0%**

### Group 2 — Special Mention (Can chu y)
- Loans overdue by 10-90 days
- Loans that have been restructured for the first time
- **Provisioning rate: 5%**

### Group 3 — Sub-standard (Duoi tieu chuan)
- Loans overdue by 91-180 days
- Loans restructured for the first time that become overdue
- **Provisioning rate: 20%**

### Group 4 — Doubtful (Nghi ngo)
- Loans overdue by 181-360 days
- Loans restructured for a second time
- **Provisioning rate: 50%**

### Group 5 — Potential Loss (Co kha nang mat von)
- Loans overdue by more than 360 days
- Loans restructured for a third time or more
- Loans where the borrower is in bankruptcy proceedings
- **Provisioning rate: 100%**

## NPL Definition

Non-performing loans (NPLs) are defined as loans classified in Groups 3, 4, or 5.

NPL ratio = (Group 3 + Group 4 + Group 5 outstanding) / Total outstanding loans.

## General Provision

In addition to specific provisions per group, credit institutions must maintain a general provision of 0.75% of total outstanding loans (all groups). This acts as a buffer against unexpected losses.

## Self-Classification Requirement

Credit institutions must perform self-classification of ALL loans on a monthly basis and report to the Credit Information Center (CIC). The CIC cross-checks with its own data and may override the institution's classification.

## Circular 31/2024 — Updated Asset Classification

Circular 31/2024/TT-NHNN replaces and supplements Circular 11/2021 with updated Internal Credit Rating System (ICRS) requirements.

### ICRS Requirements (Article 5)

Credit institutions must develop and maintain an ICRS that includes both quantitative and qualitative evaluation criteria.

**Quantitative Indicators (Financial):**
- Liquidity ratios: Current ratio, quick ratio, cash ratio
- Leverage ratios: Debt-to-equity, debt-to-total-assets, interest coverage ratio
- Profitability ratios: Gross margin, net margin, return on equity, return on assets
- Activity ratios: Inventory turnover, receivables turnover, asset turnover
- Cash flow analysis: Operating cash flow to total debt, free cash flow margin

**Qualitative Indicators (Non-Financial):**
- Management quality and experience
- Industry risk and competitive position
- Business strategy and market outlook
- Corporate governance structure
- Compliance history and regulatory track record
- Technology capability and innovation

The ICRS must be applied uniformly across the entire institution, reviewed and updated at least annually, and validated by an independent unit.

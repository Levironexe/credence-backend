# Law 14/2022/QH15 — Anti-Money Laundering

**Issued:** November 15, 2022
**Effective:** March 1, 2023
**Jurisdiction:** Vietnam

This law establishes Vietnam's AML framework aligned with FATF recommendations.

## Customer Due Diligence (CDD) Requirements

Credit institutions must perform CDD for:

1. All new customer relationships
2. Any single transaction ≥ VND 400 million (approximately USD 16,000)
3. Electronic fund transfers ≥ VND 100 million
4. When there is suspicion of money laundering regardless of amount
5. When there is doubt about previously obtained identification data

## KYC Requirements for 5 Customer Subgroups

1. **Individual customers:** Government-issued ID, address verification, source of funds
2. **Domestic legal entities:** Business registration certificate, beneficial ownership structure
3. **Foreign legal entities:** Equivalent documentation from country of origin + Vietnamese registration
4. **Correspondent banking relationships:** Enhanced due diligence including assessment of the foreign bank's AML controls
5. **Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs):** Senior management approval required for relationship, ongoing monitoring

## PEP Definition

Now includes leaders of international organizations (UN, World Bank, IMF, etc.), in addition to:
- Heads of state, heads of government, ministers, deputy ministers
- Members of parliament, supreme court judges
- Ambassadors, senior military officers (general rank and above)
- Directors of state-owned enterprises
- Family members and close associates of the above

## Suspicious Transaction Reporting (STR)

Institutions must report transactions showing any of 9 signs:

1. Transaction structure appears designed to avoid reporting thresholds
2. Customer refuses to provide required identification
3. Transaction inconsistent with customer's known business profile
4. Frequent cash transactions just below VND 400 million threshold
5. Rapid movement of funds through multiple accounts
6. Transactions with countries on FATF high-risk list
7. Customer uses multiple identities
8. Funds originate from or are destined for countries subject to UN sanctions
9. Any other signs specified by SBV guidance

## Risk-Based Customer Classification

- **Low risk:** Domestic individuals with simple transactions
- **Medium risk:** Small businesses, moderate transaction volumes
- **High risk:** PEPs, cash-intensive businesses, complex corporate structures, high-value transactions

## Record Retention

All CDD records and transaction records must be retained for at least 5 years after the end of the customer relationship.

## Annual Risk Assessment

Credit institutions must conduct an annual enterprise-wide AML risk assessment and report findings to SBV.

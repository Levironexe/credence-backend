# Law 32/2024/QH15 — Law on Credit Institutions 2024

**Issued:** January 18, 2024
**Effective:** July 1, 2024
**Jurisdiction:** Vietnam

This is the master law governing all credit institutions in Vietnam, replacing the 2010 Law on Credit Institutions. It establishes the legal framework for banks, non-bank credit institutions, microfinance institutions, and foreign bank branches operating in Vietnam.

## Lending Limits (Article 136)

Single borrower limit: 14% of charter capital and reserve funds, phasing down to 10% by January 1, 2029.

Single borrower plus related persons: 24%, phasing to 15% by 2029.

Group of related borrowers: total credit must not exceed the above limits combined.

These limits apply to all forms of credit extension: loans, guarantees, financial leasing, discount of negotiable instruments.

### Phasing Schedule

- From July 1, 2024 to December 31, 2025: 14% single / 24% group
- From January 1, 2026 to December 31, 2028: 12% single / 20% group
- From January 1, 2029 onward: 10% single / 15% group

## Prohibited Activities (Article 15)

Credit institutions must not lend to members of the Board of Directors, supervisory board members, or the General Director/CEO.

Must not extend credit on more favorable terms to insiders vs. normal customers.

Must not use charter capital to grant credit to shareholders holding 1% or more.

Must not invest in or buy shares of institutions that hold shares in it (cross-ownership prohibition).

## NPL Management

Credit institutions must establish NPL resolution departments.

Must report NPL ratio monthly to the State Bank of Vietnam (SBV).

Target NPL ratio: below 3% of total outstanding loans.

Must develop annual NPL resolution plans approved by the Board of Directors.

## Early Intervention Framework (Articles 156-162)

The SBV may place a credit institution under early intervention when:

- The institution fails to maintain the minimum capital adequacy ratio for 6 consecutive months
- NPL ratio exceeds 5% of total outstanding loans for 3 consecutive months
- The institution experiences a significant deposit withdrawal (bank run risk)
- The institution violates prudential ratios or reporting requirements repeatedly

Early intervention measures include:

- Restricting dividend payments and bonus distributions
- Requiring a restructuring plan within 60 days
- Limiting credit extension growth
- Requiring capital injection from shareholders
- Replacing management if necessary

## Special Administration (Articles 163-170)

If early intervention fails, the SBV may appoint a special administrator with powers to:

- Suspend all new lending
- Require the institution to sell assets to improve liquidity
- Negotiate merger or acquisition with a stronger institution
- In extreme cases, recommend license revocation to the Prime Minister

## Disclosure Requirements

Credit institutions must publish annual financial statements audited by an approved auditor.

Must disclose related-party transactions.

Must report to the SBV any single transaction exceeding 5% of charter capital.

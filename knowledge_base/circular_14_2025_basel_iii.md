# Circular 14/2025/TT-NHNN — Capital Adequacy Ratio (Basel III)

**Issued:** June 30, 2025
**Replaces:** Circular 41/2016/TT-NHNN
**Jurisdiction:** Vietnam

Vietnam's implementation of Basel III capital adequacy standards, bringing Vietnamese banking regulations in line with international standards.

## Minimum Capital Ratios

- **Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) ratio:** 4.5%
- **Tier 1 capital ratio:** 6.0%
- **Total Capital Adequacy Ratio (CAR):** 8.0%
- **Capital Conservation Buffer (CCB):** 2.5% (on top of minimum)
- **Counter-cyclical Buffer (CCyB):** 0-2.5% (set by SBV based on economic conditions)

Effective total requirement with buffers: 10.5% to 13.0%.

## Tier 1 Capital (CET1 + Additional Tier 1)

- Common shares (ordinary equity)
- Retained earnings
- Other comprehensive income
- Minority interests (subject to conditions)
- Excludes: goodwill, deferred tax assets, reciprocal cross-holdings

## Tier 2 Capital

- Subordinated debt (minimum 5-year maturity, amortized in last 5 years)
- General provisions (up to 1.25% of credit risk-weighted assets)
- Revaluation reserves (with SBV approval)

## Risk Weights for Credit Risk (Standardized Approach)

- Vietnamese government bonds: 0%
- Claims on SBV: 0%
- Claims on other Vietnamese banks: 20%
- Claims on foreign governments (rated AA- or above): 0%
- Retail loans: 75%
- **SME loans (below VND 25 billion): 75% (preferential treatment)**
- Residential mortgages (LTV ≤ 80%): 50%
- Residential mortgages (LTV > 80%): 75-100%
- Commercial real estate: 100-150%
- Past-due loans (> 90 days): 100-150% depending on provision coverage
- Off-balance sheet items: converted using credit conversion factors (CCF) then risk-weighted

## IRB Approach

Available from September 2025 for banks that receive SBV approval. Banks must demonstrate:
- At least 3 years of internal rating system operation
- Model validation by an independent unit
- Board of Directors approval

All banks must transition to the standardized approach by January 2030 at the latest.

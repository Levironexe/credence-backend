# Circular 39/2016/TT-NHNN — Lending Transactions of Credit Institutions

**Issued:** December 30, 2016
**Effective:** March 15, 2017
**Jurisdiction:** Vietnam

This circular is the core regulation governing lending transactions between credit institutions and customers in Vietnam.

## Borrower Conditions (Article 7)

A borrower must satisfy ALL of the following:

1. **Legal capacity:** Be a legal entity or individual with civil legal capacity
2. **Financial repayment capability:** Demonstrate ability to repay principal and interest
3. **Legitimate loan purpose:** The purpose must not violate any law
4. **Feasible business plan:** For business loans, must have a viable business plan or project proposal

## Prohibited Loan Purposes (Article 8)

Credit institutions must NOT lend for:

1. Trading in goods/services prohibited by law
2. Buying/using goods/services that are banned
3. Capital contribution/share purchase in another credit institution (circular lending)
4. Repaying a loan at another credit institution (loan-to-repay-loan prohibition), unless the refinancing is approved by SBV

Additional prohibitions added by Circular 06/2023:

5. Placing deposits at another credit institution using borrowed funds
6. Lending to invest in corporate bonds where the issuer's purpose is restricted
7. Lending to invest in shares/equity in cases that circumvent lending limits
8. Lending for real estate speculation in restricted zones designated by the government

## Interest Rate Framework (Article 13)

- **Short-term VND loans:** Maximum rate capped by SBV (currently 4.0% per annum for priority sectors)
- **Priority sectors:** agriculture, exports, SMEs, high-tech industries, supporting industries
- **Medium and long-term loans:** Market-driven, negotiated between lender and borrower
- **Interest rate adjustments:** Must notify borrower at least 10 business days in advance for variable-rate loans

## Loan Types by Term

- **Short-term:** up to 12 months — for working capital, seasonal needs
- **Medium-term:** over 12 months to 60 months — for equipment, expansion
- **Long-term:** over 60 months — for infrastructure, real estate, large projects

## 9 Lending Methods (Article 4)

1. Lump-sum loan (cho vay tung lan): Single disbursement, single repayment schedule
2. Revolving credit line (cho vay theo han muc tin dung): Pre-approved limit, draw down as needed
3. Installment loan (cho vay tra gop): Fixed periodic payments
4. Syndicated loan (cho vay hop von): Multiple lenders participate
5. Overdraft facility (cho vay thau chi): Linked to demand deposit account
6. Financial leasing (cho vay theo phuong thuc khac): Equipment/asset-based
7. Loan based on credit limit (cho vay theo du no): Balance-based lending
8. Factoring (bao thanh toan): Advance against receivables
9. Other methods approved by SBV

## Loan Agreement Requirements (Article 23)

Every loan agreement MUST include:

- Loan amount, currency, term
- Interest rate (fixed or variable) and calculation method
- Disbursement schedule and repayment schedule
- Collateral description (if any)
- Rights and obligations of both parties
- Default interest rate (not exceeding 150% of the contract rate)
- Events of default and remedies
- Dispute resolution mechanism

## Debt Rescheduling and Overdue Debt (Articles 19-20)

Borrower may request debt rescheduling if unable to repay on time.

Rescheduling must be requested BEFORE the due date.

Overdue debt is automatically converted at the overdue interest rate (up to 150% of contract rate).

Restructured loans may be reclassified to a higher risk group under Circular 11/2021.
